IPhO 1967 Problem 4

A Classic Physics Olympiad Problem

The Problem

A closed vessel with volume $V_{0}=10$ liters contains dry air in the normal conditions ($t_{0}=0^{\circ}C$, $p_{0}=1$ atm). In some moment 3 g of water were added to the vessel and the system was warmed up to $t=100^{\circ}C$.

Find the pressure in the vessel. Discuss assumptions you made to solve the problem.

The Solution

The solution starts by considering the state of the water added to the vessel. We first assume that all the water evaporates.

Assumptions

  • The added water completely evaporates. The density of the water vapor ($0.300 \text{ g/l}$) is less than the density of saturated vapor at $100^{\circ}C$ ($0.597 \text{ g/l}$), so no liquid water remains.
  • Both air and the unsaturated water vapor behave as ideal gases.

Applying Physics Laws

According to Dalton's Law, the total pressure ($p$) in the vessel is the sum of the partial pressures of the air ($p_a$) and the water vapor ($p_v$):

$$p = p_{a} + p_{v}$$

Pressure of the Air ($p_a$)

Since the volume is constant, we can apply Gay-Lussac's Law to the air. The pressure is proportional to the absolute temperature:

$$p_{a} = p_{0}\left(\frac{273 + t}{273}\right)$$

Pressure of the Water Vapor ($p_v$)

We can find the pressure of the water vapor using the ideal gas law:

$$\frac{p_{v}V_{0}}{273+t} = \frac{m}{\mu}R$$

where $m$ is the mass of the vapor, $\mu$ is the molecular mass of water, and $R$ is the universal gas constant. Rearranging for $p_v$:

$$p_{v} = \frac{m}{\mu}R\left(\frac{273+t}{V_{0}}\right)$$

Final Pressure Calculation

Substituting the expressions for $p_a$ and $p_v$ back into the total pressure equation, we get the final formula:

$$p = p_{0}\frac{273+t}{273} + \frac{m}{\mu}R\frac{273+t}{V_{0}}$$

Using the given numerical values:

  • $p_0 = 1$ atm
  • $t = 100^{\circ}C$
  • $m = 3$ g
  • $V_0 = 10$ l

The numerical solution gives:

$$p = (1.366 + 0.516) \text{ atm} \approx 1.88 \text{ atm}$$
