Problem & Solution

Problem

A boat moves relative to water with a velocity which is $n=2.0$ times less than the river flow velocity. At what angle to the stream direction must the boat move to minimize drifting?

Vector Components and Drifting

We can set up a coordinate system where the x-axis is in the direction of the river flow and the y-axis is perpendicular to it (across the river).

The river's velocity is entirely in the x-direction: $\vec{v_r} = v_r \hat{i}$

The boat's velocity relative to the water has both x and y components, dependent on the angle $\alpha$: $\vec{v_b} = v_b \cos\alpha \hat{i} + v_b \sin\alpha \hat{j}$

The net velocity of the boat relative to the bank is the vector sum of these two velocities: $\vec{v_{net}} = \vec{v_r} + \vec{v_b}$

$$\vec{v_{net}} = (v_r + v_b \cos\alpha) \hat{i} + (v_b \sin\alpha) \hat{j}$$

The "drifting" we want to minimize is the displacement in the x-direction (downstream). To minimize this drift for a given river width $W$, we must find the angle that minimizes the time to cross the river while also counteracting the river flow.

The time $t$ to cross the river of width $W$ is determined by the y-component of the net velocity: $$t = \frac{W}{v_{net, y}} = \frac{W}{v_b \sin\alpha}$$

The downstream drift $D$ is the distance covered in the x-direction during this time: $$D = v_{net, x} \times t = (v_r + v_b \cos\alpha) \times \frac{W}{v_b \sin\alpha}$$

$$D = W \left( \frac{v_r}{v_b \sin\alpha} + \frac{v_b \cos\alpha}{v_b \sin\alpha} \right)$$

$$D = W \left( \frac{v_r}{v_b} \csc\alpha + \cot\alpha \right)$$

Using the given ratio $\frac{v_r}{v_b} = n = 2.0$: $$D(\alpha) = W \left( 2.0 \csc\alpha + \cot\alpha \right)$$

Minimizing the Drift

To find the angle $\alpha$ that minimizes the drift, we take the derivative of the drift $D(\alpha)$ with respect to $\alpha$ and set it equal to zero.

$$\frac{dD}{d\alpha} = \frac{d}{d\alpha} \left[ W \left( 2.0 \csc\alpha + \cot\alpha \right) \right]$$

$$\frac{dD}{d\alpha} = W (-2.0 \csc\alpha \cot\alpha - \csc^2\alpha) = 0$$

Since $W$ is a non-zero constant, we can divide by it. We can also divide by $\csc\alpha$, as $\sin\alpha \ne 0$ for a valid crossing:

$$-2.0 \cot\alpha - \csc\alpha = 0$$

$$2.0 \cot\alpha = -\csc\alpha$$

$$2.0 \frac{\cos\alpha}{\sin\alpha} = - \frac{1}{\sin\alpha}$$

Assuming $\sin\alpha \ne 0$, we can cancel it out: $$2.0 \cos\alpha = -1$$

$$\cos\alpha = -\frac{1}{2.0} = -0.5$$

Finally, we find the angle $\alpha$: $$\alpha = \arccos(-0.5) = 120^\circ$$

This angle of $120^\circ$ is measured with respect to the direction of the river's flow.

Answer

To minimize drifting, the boat must move at an angle of $120^\circ$ to the stream direction.
